Shooting Line

  1. Shooting line– this is a technique that uses the line hand and is an essential skill.
    1. Introduction
      1. When fish are beyond your normal casting range and you need extra distance, that's when you need to shoot line. It looks like this...
    2. Instructor demonstrates
      1. Demonstrate shooting line
    3. Instructor emphasizes key element to shooting line
      1. Shooting line means to extend extra line at THE END of the presentation stroke.
        1. This means shooting line is done after the rod is stopped.
    4. Technique with visual and verbal description
      1. Start with line straight ahead on the water with tip low.
      2. Strip abut 6 ft of line off the reel.
      3. Grasp the line with the line hand at the same level as the rod hand.
      4. Separate your hands by about 12 inches.
        1. The goal is to move the line hand in unison with the rod hand-on a parallel path-during the casting stroke. (emphasize thumbnail to thumbnail)
      5. Initiate overhead false casts, maintaining the grasp (anchor) on the line with line hand.
      6. Shoot the line and present the fly to the target on the 3rd false cast.
        1. Once the rod had stopped on the presentation cast, release the grip on the line.
        2. The key here is to “Stop and then let go” It’s two separate steps. It is not “Stopletgo.”
        3. Form an "O" using the thumb and index finger of your line hand for the line to slide through.
          1. This helps prevent the line from tangling on the reel, rod butt, or other items.
          2. Big advantage to this is that you have instant line control by closing your hand.
        4. When the fly lands on the water, control the line with line hand and place line between handle and index finger of rod hand.
        5. Now start to strip in line by grasping the line just below the anchor point and pull it in and you slightly release the pinch against the handle.

  4. Challenges and Fixes (anticipate challenges) aka troubleshooting
    1. Difficulty with timing of release of line.
      1. Remind student to first stop the rod and then release the line.
      2. Maybe demonstrate letting the line go simultaneous to the stop while saying “Stopletgo.” It will unbend the rod prematurely and the loop will fall apart.
      3. Tell the students that it is better to let go too late rather than too early.
    2. False casting with too long of a line. Unable to load rod properly to shoot line.
      1. Have student cast false cast a shorter line. Tell them they can lengthen their cast when they have mastered control with the shorter distance. They will spook fish otherwise!
