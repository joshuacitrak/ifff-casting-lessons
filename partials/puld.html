Pick up and Lay Down (PULD) Cast

  1. Introduction to cast.
  2. Instructor demonstrates cast.
  3. Instructor describes the cast
      4. The cast consists of a single back cast and a single forward cast, beginning and ending with the line on the water.  It is the starting point for learning all overhead casts.
  4. Instructor states purposes of the PULD cast.
    1. The purpose of the cast is to teach rod loading and unloading, the pause length between back cast and forward cast, and how to control the size of the loops
    2. In fishing this cast achieves greater accuracy than the roll cast and allows the quick and efficient repositioning of the fly on the water.
  1. Set-up for the cast.
    1. Instructor pulls about 20 feet of line beyond rod tip (a little more than twice the rod length).
    2. Do not use line hand during the cast.
    3. Instructor hands the rod to the student.
    4. Show the student how to pinch line between their index finger and rod handle.
    5. Student stands square to the target. It is OK to drop one foot back, whatever is comfortable for the student.
  1. Student performs the cast with kinesthetic assistance from instructor. This means that the instructor holds the rod either on top of the student's hand or just above the cork grip.
    1. Start with straight line and rod tip in the water. Use a roll cast to straighten out the line.
      1. Use command: ROD TIP IN THE WATER
  2. With a firm wrist, smoothly lift the line-leader connection from the water (point out this important visual cue to the student)
    1. Use command: LIFT
  3. Without pausing, smoothly accelerate the rod to a crisp stop at the 1:00 o'clock position. This acceleration begins when the line-leader connection leaves the water.
    1. Use command: ACCELERATE TO A STOP
  4. Pause, at the 1:00 o'clock position, to let the line unroll. Line should look like a candy cane on its back.
    1. Use command: PAUSE
  5. Initiate forward cast by smoothly accelerating the rod to an abrupt stop at 10:00 o'clock.
    1. Use command: ACCELERATE TO A STOP
  6. Drop rod tip to the water AFTER stopping the rod and the line is in front of the rod tip.
    1. Use command: DROP
  1. Student now performs the cast without kinesthetic assistance from the instructor while instructor repeats the verbal commands.
  1. Troubleshooting the cast.
    1. Line is ripped off the water creating a disturbance and causing the back cast to recoil, creating slack in the line.  Fix by beginning lift slowly while increasing pressure on rod tip so rod bends gradually during acceleration.
    2. A pause is made between lift and stroke creating slack in the line.  Fix with a no-pause transition between lift and stroke.
    3. Wide loop on the back cast.  Fix by accelerating to a brisk stop with rod tip traveling back and upward as in a stabbing motion and stopping at 1:00 o'clock.
    4. Very wide loop on forward cast.  Fix is to stop the rod higher or apply more force to load the rod.
    5. A tailing loop results on forward cast.  Fix is to stop the rod lower or lessen power application with a controlled stop.
